WOUND
TURMERIC
The curcumin found in turmeric can help wounds heal by decreasing inflammation and oxidation. It also lowers the response of your body to cutaneous wounds. This results in your wounds healing more quickly.
Application of turmeric paste or even turmeric powder over the affected area provides help since turmeric is known to contain antibacterial principles. A piece of raw turmeric (unpowdered) or alternatively, a teaspoonful of it boiled in a glass of milk taken internally offers relief from pain associated with wounds.
WOUND
NEEM
Neem leaves have been traditionally used to cleanse wounds, prevent infections, and speed up healing. You can apply fresh neem leaf paste or neem oil to the wound for faster healing and infection control.
Crush fresh neem leaves into a paste and apply directly to the wound. Alternatively, neem oil can be used.
WOUND
ALOE VERA
Aloe vera is widely known for its soothing effects and its ability to speed up the healing process of burns, cuts, and other types of wounds.
Properties: Cooling, hydrating, anti-inflammatory, and promotes cell regeneration.
How to use: Extract fresh aloe vera gel from the leaf and apply directly to the wound.
WOUND
HONEY
Honey is a natural wound healer, helping to prevent infection, reduce inflammation, and speed up the healing process.
Properties: Antimicrobial, soothing, and promotes tissue regeneration.
How to use: Apply raw honey directly to the wound. It can also be mixed with turmeric for enhanced effects.
